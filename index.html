The path to Software Developer

This timeline traces the invisible presence of key moments and transitions in my life, highlighting how computers, digital art, photography, and product engineering have shaped my journey to become Front-End Developer. The timeline reflects my evolving skills and mindset, fueled by a passion for creativity and innovation in the digital world. Ultimately, it demonstrates how technology has the power to transform lives and inspire new directions.

About Me

In childhood I have developed an interest to fine arts, handcraft and sand sculpting. Spending my free time painting, sketching, building sand pyramids of various shapes and forms, working with clay, plastics and metal to repair broken toys and create new gadgets.
I also loved to teach myself how to use new home appliances and its functions via handbooks in English language. As a result I taught myself English with the use of vocabulary.

Early 90s

Since my father was radio officer on sea vessels I got my first Sega video game console as birthday present as it was released. This moment marked an incredible time diving deep in games and learning them. Later on I got my hands on Sony Playstation, a giant leap towards 3D gaming experience.
I remember myself observing graphic elements in games and later on repainting them on paper.

Early 2000s

In early 2000s I got my first personal computer with Windows Millenium. This opened my eyes to a new range of online games and especially, the use of internet. It was the moment when I started to investigate computer software, files, graphics and use of information.
I was reading online how to use computer and eventually taught how to reinstall windows, download and install software, burn CDs, and replace hardware parts on my own.

Mid 2000s

Graduating from high school and not seeing how my interests and skills can aid me choosing career I went to China to see my father. At that time I was engaged with digital photography and photo editing.
Eventually, I found myself working for food sourcing company in China for 2 years as IT Support Specialist and Field Service Technician. I used to draw in CAD, set-up satellite call stations, install and configure vessel navigation software and aid in office with IT questions. And again, all these were self-taught disciplines.

2008-2012

I returned home and got Bachelor Degree in European Business and gained understanding of business fundamentals, communication, management, finance, economics, European law, French and a little of Spanish languages.
My interest in night photography and use of photo editing tools made me thinking how to make web page to display images online. But I got confused about complexity of programming, and personal biases of not knowing math kicked in.
As a result my friend stepped in and made www.light-fusion.com as his pet project on PHP.

2012-2017

Throughout this period I worked in banking as client support and other financial firms as client manager sourcing clients and connecting secure payment infrastructure to e-commerce platforms of their choice.
It was time when I noticed how it is convenient and interesting to have e-commerce business online.
At that time, I also got passionate with motorcycle riding and obtained an interest servicing and repairing them.

2018-2023

In 2018 my life got an eventual turn and a road accident brought me to study welding and do repairs of my motorcycle on my own.
I got my TIG welding certification and ended up being employed as project manager at the place of my practice.
As a result I gained experience in industrial manufacturing, civil engineering, metal fabrication, and production, as later on I had additional position as manager of manufacturing. These roles were of high responsibility, a lot of stress, and teamwork dependent.

2023 and on

As a result of COVID and further war conflict in 2022, manufacturing sector had seen decline and eventually in a year I could no longer find a position.
This had led me to go back in my timeline, review my personality, obtained skills, competencies, acquired knowledge, passions, engagements and involvements which I combined with certain expectations of my future position and decided to try and become Software Developer.
As a result I finished course on Manual Testing and had intensive course on Automation Testing as complimentary and introductory part to software development process. And further on I enrolled and currently keep learning Front-End Development self-taught course as it fully reflects my interests and passions.
